## About

I'm a **radiologist** and **healthtech engineer** building the infrastructure that makes AI-assisted radiology a clinical reality. Currently at [**Laudos.AI**](https://laudos.ai), where I work at the intersection of medical imaging, autonomous AI agents, and production healthcare systems.

My work lives at the boundary between radiology workflow and software engineering — I understand both the clinical requirements of a radiologist reading CTs at 2am and the engineering constraints of building reliable, compliant systems that touch patient data.

- **Day job:** Radiologist + Co-building AI-powered radiology reporting infrastructure
- **Focus:** Autonomous AI agents for radiology (PACS · EHR · DICOM · FHIR · worklists · critical findings)
- **Stack:** Python · TypeScript · medical imaging pipelines · LLM orchestration
- **Currently shipping:** ATLAS — autonomous agent infrastructure for radiology

## Approach

Radiology is one of the few medical specialties where the core product is a **structured text document** derived from images — which means it's uniquely positioned for AI transformation. I build systems that augment radiologists, not replace them: catching what gets missed at volume, structuring what gets lost in prose, and routing what gets delayed in broken workflows.

Good medical AI is boring. It's reliable, explainable, auditable, and it fails gracefully. That's what I build.
